Data Visualization — A Case Study

Siddarthathentu
Sep 15 · 4 min read

A simple explanatory analysis of a popular Twitter account WeRateDogs

Introduction

Who doesn’t love dogs? But WeRateDogs takes it up a notch. Founded in 2015 by a college student Matt Nelson, this hilarious Twitter account rates people’s dogs with a humorous comment about the dog. It is well known for its unique rating system. For most of the ratings. the denominator is 10. However, the numerator is almost always greater than 10 (11/10, 14/10, 15/10). Although some people might argue that ratings are supposed to up to 10/10, WeRateDogs disagree. They believe that all dogs deserve 10 and more than a 10.

WeRateDogs received international media coverage when it’s founder spawned the catchphrase “They’re good dogs, Brent”, while shutting down a person having an issue with its rating system. This became one of the biggest memes of 2016 and as of now, the account has garnered around 8 million followers.

The documentation for how the data was gathered, assessed and cleaned is available here. This post solely focuses on visualization and I encourage you to check out the code as it’s a good exercise to practice data wrangling.

For any good explanatory analysis, the most important part starts with asking the right questions. Now for a twitter data for dogs, let’s quickly come up with a few curious questions.

  1. About which breed are most of the tweets about?
  2. Which dog breed has the most number of retweets i.e most popular?
  3. Which size of dogs are more popular? Puppers? Floofers?
  4. Which breed of dogs are less talked about?

Let’s try and answer these questions one by one. I used python’s Seaborn for data visualization.

Which breed of dog is most of the tweets about?

import seaborn as sby = final_df['breed'].value_counts().iloc[:5]
x = y.index

sb.barplot(y,x,orient='h',palette='viridis');

Which dog breed has the most number of retweets i.e most popular?

final_df[['breed','retweet_count']].groupby('breed',as_index=True).sum().sort_values(by='retweet_count',ascending=False).iloc[:5].plot(kind="bar",color=['#5cb85c','#5bc0de','#d9534f'])
plt.xticks(rotation=-15)
plt.legend()
plt.ylabel("Number of retweets")
plt.xlabel("Name of breed")
plt.title("Retweet Count vs Breed")
plt.show();

Golden retrievers are the most retweeted and most popular in WeRateDogs archive. Why not? Look at him! He is magnificent.

Conclusion

There are ton of other analysis that can be done. For example

  1. How has the tweet traffic been, since the inception of this account? Is it still popular?
  2. Are the spikes in traffic happening in any particular season?
  3. On average, what is the rating give to a Golden Retriever? Is the author biased?
  4. What is the trend of retweets? Is it decreasing? Are followers happy with WeRateDog’s tweets?

In any Data Analysis, gathering , assessing and cleaning are just one side of the story. Our ability to raise questions, spin stories, develop insights, predict trends and most pressingly take good decisions is what makes us stand out. This is my first post in medium. I hope you guys enjoy it.
